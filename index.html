How It Works

Simple. Transparent. Fair.

From registration to claim settlement — every step is automated and inflation-aware.

1
Client Registration
Your details are captured with full economic profiling — currency preference, income level, risk classification.
2
Asset Valuation
Assets are valued in ZiG and USD simultaneously. Historical policies are converted from ZWL/RTGS using exact RBZ rates.
3
Premium Calculation
Premiums are calculated with an inflation loading component that adjusts monthly. You always pay a fair, current-market rate.
4
Fair Claim Settlement
Claims are settled at inflation-adjusted values. Severity classification ensures the payout reflects the real cost of the loss.

Currency
Zimbabwe's Monetary History

17 Years · 5 Currencies · One Platform

RiskShield tracks every currency change so no historical policy is ever lost to conversion confusion.

2009 – 2014
Multi-Currency Era
USD · ZAR · BWP · 1:1 parity
Dollarization ends hyperinflation. Stability returns. Insurance penetration at 60%.
Stable
2014 – 2016
Bond Notes (1:1 USD)
1 Bond = 1 USD (official)
Surrogate currency introduced. Initial stability maintained. Confidence begins to waver.
Transitional
2016 – 2019
Bond Notes Devalued → RTGS
1 USD = 2.5 → 15 local units
Bond notes lose value. Cash shortages. RTGS electronic currency introduced. Insurance penetration drops to 32%.
Crisis
2019 – 2023
RTGS → ZWL Hyperinflation
1 USD = 80 → 8,000 ZWL
500%+ inflation. Currency chaos. Insurance claim payouts worthless by settlement date. Trust collapses to 15%.
Hyperinflation
2024 – Present
ZiG — Zimbabwe Gold
1 USD = 26.90 ZiG (stable)
Gold-backed currency launched April 2024. Single-digit inflation achieved. 715+ days of stability. Insurance trust rebuilding.
